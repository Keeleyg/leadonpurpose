Core Principles

The Approach

Practices for intentional leadership in a chaotic world where the only constant is change.

01

Knowing Over Believing

Knowledge holds provisional conclusions based on evidence. Belief treats conclusions as fixed points around which evidence must be arranged. The difference determines how you respond to new information.

02

The Holy Trinity

Purpose answers why you exist. Values guide how you decide. Vision paints where you're heading. Together, they form the cultural bedrock from which everything else grows.

03

Culture Gives Birth to Strategy

Culture doesn't eat strategy for breakfast. Culture gives birth to strategy. When strategy is aligned and born from culture, they become a unified force that makes scaling inevitable.

04

Second-Order Leadership

First-order leaders are experts with answers. Second-order leaders are coaches who ask better questions. The shift happens when you stop trying to know everything and start trying to understand everyone.

05

Evolution Over Expansion

Growth isn't strategy. It's often a failure of imagination. The question isn't "how do I grow?" The question is "how do I stay relevant to the people I choose to serve?"

06

The Courage to Face Reality

It's a damning observation of our times that speaking truth requires courage. Leaders stand apart by speaking the unspoken truth. Evidence is your armour, your legitimacy, your protection.

About Gavin Keeley

Partner, Circle Leadership Global · Adjunct Professor, QUT

Gavin Keeley is a Partner at Circle Leadership Global and an Adjunct Professor at Queensland University of Technology, with a personal purpose to achieve what is thought to be impossible.

Born in the industrial West Midlands in the United Kingdom, Gavin's career began with a sliding door moment that led him from an aspiring astrophysicist to a pioneering computer scientist leading global transformations of the PC, the internet, Cloud Computing, and AI all years before they were each mainstream. He's held executive roles at Suncorp, Aviva PLC, and founded technology companies across four continents.

His philosophy—"constantly curious"—has guided four decades of leadership. From delivering impossible insurance products in 30 days (when years was normal) to leading campaigns that transformed Queensland's digital infrastructure, Gavin has consistently challenged what's possible.

"I've spent forty years watching leaders fail in predictable ways—not because they lacked intelligence, but because they were never trained to lead. I wrote this book because I got tired of staying quiet about it."

Today, through Circle Leadership, he works to enable 90 million purpose-driven leaders with a vision to create a world where work positively impacts people and the planet.
